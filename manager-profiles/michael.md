# Michael

## Profile Summary

Michael is the long-view builder. He treats the startup as a multi-year asset build, values age-adjusted growth curves, and is willing to lag in immediate certainty if the long-term talent base improves.

## Tendencies

- Patience with poor performance: High, especially for young players
- Veteran versus youth balance: Youth leaning
- Minor-league draft appetite: Very high
- Preferred minors depth: High-A and deeper if the long-term talent base justifies the wait
- Category adjustment aggression: Low to moderate
- Free-agent activity: Measured
- Injury handling: Willing to stash meaningful long-term assets
- Dynasty horizon: Long term

## Primary Data Reliance

- Leans most on: dynasty rankings, age curve, prospect information, skill indicators
- Uses as tie-breakers: development runway, upper-level proximity, underlying skills beneath ugly surface lines
- Discounts relative to others: short-term market ADP, older win-now producers, role-only veterans without long-term anchor value

## Draft Strategy

- places a premium on young MLB players and upper-level prospects with long runway value
- is willing to pass on some aging producers to secure multi-year core assets
- treats the startup draft as the foundation of a long-term talent pipeline
- is comfortable taking prospects who are further from the majors when the upside and age curve are strong
- prefers skills growth and age-adjusted upside over one-year ceiling
- accepts some short-term pain if the long-term roster value improves
- is the most willing manager to rank a 24-year-old skills-growth bat ahead of a 31-year-old bankable producer in the same tier

## Sunday Decision Style

- gives prospects and early-career hitters extra time before demotion or replacement
- avoids sacrificing long-term upside for marginal one-week gains
- will still optimize the active lineup, but protects core young assets
- uses veterans mainly when they provide clear category stability or ratio protection
