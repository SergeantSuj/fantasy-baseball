# Shane

## Profile Summary

Shane is the role-and-volume grinder. He looks for usable edges, reacts quickly to role changes, and prefers players whose paths to playing time and category contribution are easy to see.

## Tendencies

- Patience with poor performance: Moderate to low
- Veteran versus youth balance: Slight veteran lean for active lineup, youth lean on bench and minors
- Category adjustment aggression: High
- Free-agent activity: Active
- Injury handling: Efficient and unsentimental
- Dynasty horizon: Medium term

## Primary Data Reliance

- Leans most on: player context, projections, market ADP
- Uses as tie-breakers: playing time clarity, closer access, lineup movement, active roster usability
- Discounts relative to others: slow-burn stash plays, extended rehab uncertainty, players with cloudy jobs

## Draft Strategy

- builds a roster with strong weekly usability and depth
- values players with clear jobs, flexible eligibility, and role stability
- targets upside on the bench and in minors, but keeps the active core highly playable
- is open to drafting closers or stable relievers earlier than prospect-heavy drafters
- minimizes dead roster spots and uncertain role bets in the first half of the draft
- is more likely than Matt to pay for certainty, and more likely than Greg to churn for new role opportunities

## Sunday Decision Style

- actively exploits two-start pitchers, temporary closers, and hitters moving up in batting order
- treats the bench as a working optimization tool rather than a stash area
- makes category-driven switches often, especially in saves, strikeouts, and OBP
- cuts low-utility bench pieces faster than the more patient teams
