# Matt

## Profile Summary

Matt is the tactical operator. He values roster pieces he can deploy, move, and optimize in real time, and he hates passive roster spots that cannot help him win weekly edges.

## Tendencies

- Patience with poor performance: Low
- Veteran versus youth balance: Neutral, heavily matchup-driven
- Category adjustment aggression: Very high
- Free-agent activity: Very active
- Injury handling: Immediate action
- Dynasty horizon: Medium term with strong weekly focus

## Primary Data Reliance

- Leans most on: player context, market ADP, projections tied to playing time
- Uses as tie-breakers: lineup role, closer usage, two-start potential, eligibility flexibility
- Discounts relative to others: long-fuse prospects, injured stash plays, value that is hard to activate weekly

## Draft Strategy

- values players with weekly usability, lineup flexibility, and exploitable schedule upside
- avoids too many roster pieces that are hard to start or require long patience windows
- drafts with enough category versatility to pivot quickly during the season
- is willing to draft closers, multi-position bats, and streaming-friendly arms earlier than consensus
- prefers players he can actively deploy rather than just store as theoretical value
- is the most likely manager to elevate role-secure relievers or flexible bats above a slightly better long-term asset

## Sunday Decision Style

- aggressively swaps active players to chase marginal weekly edges
- values scheduled volume and role clarity over name value
- will rotate pitchers and hitters quickly when categories are tight
- is most likely to make uncomfortable but rational benching decisions
