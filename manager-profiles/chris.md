# Chris

## Profile Summary

Chris is the consensus builder. He prefers balanced rosters, does not want to lose categories through over-specialization, and trusts overlap between multiple sources more than any one loud signal.

## Tendencies

- Patience with poor performance: Moderate
- Veteran versus youth balance: Even split
- Prospect stash appetite: Moderate but selective
- Preferred minors depth: Upper minors, mostly AA before the very deep developmental tiers
- Category adjustment aggression: Moderate
- Free-agent activity: Selective and practical
- Injury handling: Conservative with longer-term injuries, active on short-term replacements
- Dynasty horizon: Medium term

## Primary Data Reliance

- Leans most on: blended dynasty rankings, blended market ADP, projection balance
- Uses as tie-breakers: multi-position eligibility, age, broad category balance
- Discounts relative to others: single-source hype, thin prospect helium, one-category specialists

## Team-Building Strategy

- targets a balanced roster without obvious category holes
- prefers multi-category hitters over one-dimensional specialists
- mixes proven MLB production with a few younger upside pieces rather than going all-in on either side
- waits on extreme risk unless the value discount is substantial
- treats prospects as complementary assets, not the core of the roster
- prefers minor-league stashes who are close enough to contribute without forcing a long development queue
- prefers players supported by both dynasty rank and market price rather than outliers in only one source

## Sunday Decision Style

- gives proven players a little more runway than a highly reactive manager would
- will bench a struggling player if role, lineup spot, or playing time declines
- prefers balanced category contributors over one-category specialists unless a category is clearly deficient
- makes lineup changes to gain points, but avoids chasing tiny short-term edges
