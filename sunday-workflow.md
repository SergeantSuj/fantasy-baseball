# Sunday Workflow

This document defines the weekly operating cycle for managing each team across the two recurring checkpoints in the league week:

- Sunday evening, when rosters must be set before the next scoring week locks on Sunday at 12:00 AM
- Monday morning, when stats from the previous scoring week are added to season totals and standings are updated

The goal is to maximize each roster's expected point gain for the coming week while keeping all teams legal under the league rules.

Manager-specific judgment calls should be guided by [manager-profiles.md](manager-profiles.md).

## Weekly Checkpoints

The league week has two distinct operating checkpoints.

### 1. Sunday Evening: Set Next Week's Roster

Use the Sunday process to prepare each team for the scoring period that begins at Sunday 12:00 AM.

This is the decision window for:

- lineup choices
- benching and activation decisions
- IR moves
- minors compliance
- free-agent replacements and short-term category targeting

### 2. Monday Morning: Post Prior Week Results

Use the Monday process to roll the completed scoring week into the season totals.

This is the accounting window for:

- adding each active player's prior-week stats to team totals
- recalculating category standings
- updating total roto points
- verifying that the standings reflect the newly posted week correctly

## Weekly Objective

For each team, complete the Sunday lineup process before lock and the Monday accounting process after the scoring period closes so that:

- injured players are moved out of active slots when possible
- eligible injured players are placed on IR
- underperforming or low-opportunity players are challenged by available free agents
- minor league eligibility is kept legal
- active lineup choices reflect the team's category needs for the coming week
- completed weekly stats are added to the correct season totals
- standings reflect the previous scoring week's results
- all teams enter the week with a legal and optimized active roster

## Order of Operations

Run the Sunday process in this order for every team:

1. Check roster legality
2. Process injuries and IR moves
3. Review minor league eligibility and prospect value
4. Review recent player performance and role changes
5. Review free agent alternatives
6. Review category strengths and deficiencies
7. Set the optimal active lineup for the week
8. Confirm the roster is legal before weekly lock

## 1. Check Roster Legality

Before making optimization decisions, confirm that the roster is legal.

Review:

- active lineup has all required positions filled
- bench does not exceed 8 players
- minor league slots do not exceed 15 players
- IR contains only players who are platform-eligible for IR
- any prospect who has lost minor league eligibility is not left in a minors slot

If the roster is illegal, correct legality first before making performance-based decisions.

## 2. Process Injuries and IR Moves

Review injury news, MLB transaction status, and expected availability for the coming scoring week.

For each injured player:

- if IR-eligible, move the player to IR immediately
- if not IR-eligible and projected to miss most or all of the week, remove the player from the active lineup
- if day-to-day, compare expected playing time against available bench or free-agent alternatives

When opening a roster spot through IR:

- promote the best bench or minor league replacement who is eligible and likely to contribute
- if internal replacements are weak, check free agency before finalizing the lineup

## 3. Review Minor League Eligibility and Performance

For every player in a minor league slot, verify both eligibility and utility.

Eligibility review:

- hitters must remain below 130 MLB at-bats
- pitchers must remain below 50 MLB innings pitched
- players who exceed the threshold must be promoted or otherwise made roster-compliant before lock

Performance review:

- identify prospects earning more playing time or nearing promotion
- identify minor leaguers whose value has materially improved and may justify an active or bench role soon
- identify stagnant or declining prospects who may no longer deserve protection if a better free agent or prospect is available

Decision rules:

- promote minor leaguers who are both eligible for activation and likely to help this week
- retain high-upside prospects if they still justify a minors slot
- cut low-value minors only if a clearly better acquisition is available and the move improves the roster

## 4. Review Recent Performance and Role Changes

For all active and bench players, review the most recent information that affects expected weekly output.

Check:

- last 7 to 14 days of performance
- lineup position for hitters
- platoon risk
- expected number of games this week
- two-start week potential for starters
- bullpen role stability for relievers
- changes in closer usage
- call-ups, demotions, suspensions, and rest patterns

Flag players who are poor starts for the upcoming week because of:

- injury risk
- loss of role
- poor playing-time outlook
- severe slump combined with weak opportunity
- bad schedule context relative to alternatives

## 5. Review Free Agent Alternatives

For any weak active or bench spot, compare the current player against available free agents.

Target free agents who improve one or more of the following:

- expected playing time this week
- category contribution in an area of team need
- role security
- short-term schedule advantage
- injury replacement value

Examples:

- add hitters with strong OBP skills if the team needs OBP support
- add base-stealers if the team is lagging in stolen bases
- add closers or primary save sources if saves are weak
- add likely two-start pitchers if strikeouts or wins need support and ratio risk is acceptable
- avoid empty-volume players who add plate appearances or innings without meaningful category benefit

Replacement rule:

- replace a poorly performing player only when the projected weekly gain from the free agent is meaningfully better than the current roster option

## 6. Review Category Strengths and Deficiencies

Before setting the lineup, evaluate where the team stands in the roto table.

For each team, classify each category as one of:

- strong
- competitive
- deficient

Suggested approach:

- strong: category lead is stable enough that protecting it matters more than forcing extra volume
- competitive: modest gains this week could move the team up the standings
- deficient: the team is materially behind and needs targeted roster choices to gain points

### Hitting Review

Assess:

- Runs
- Home Runs
- RBI
- Stolen Bases
- OBP

Questions:

- does the team need more plate appearances or better rate quality
- should the lineup prioritize speed over power this week
- should low-OBP bats be benched in favor of steadier on-base hitters

### Pitching Review

Assess:

- Wins
- Strikeouts
- Saves
- ERA
- WHIP

Questions:

- can the team gain in volume categories without damaging ratios
- should the team favor relievers to protect ERA and WHIP
- should the team favor starters to chase wins and strikeouts
- is the team close enough in saves to justify activating more relievers

## 7. Set the Optimal Active Lineup

Use the category review to choose the active lineup that best fits the coming week.

Guiding principle:

- start the lineup that creates the highest expected roto-point improvement, not simply the lineup with the biggest names

### Hitter Decisions

Prefer hitters who offer:

- more games scheduled this week
- better lineup placement
- stronger OBP contribution when OBP is a contested category
- stolen-base upside when SB is a target category
- power and run-production opportunity when HR and RBI are moveable categories

Bench hitters who:

- are likely to sit multiple games
- are in a weak-side platoon
- have poor OBP and limited category upside
- offer redundant category strength when the team needs help elsewhere

### Pitcher Decisions

Prefer pitchers who offer:

- stable role and expected workload
- a likely two-start week when chasing Wins or Strikeouts
- strong ratio protection when ERA and WHIP are critical
- reliable save opportunities when Saves are contested

Bench pitchers who:

- face severe matchup risk if ratio protection is more valuable than volume
- have uncertain roles
- are unlikely to produce saves, wins, or strikeouts at a meaningful level

### Lineup Switching Logic

Make active-lineup switches when at least one of the following is true:

- the replacement meaningfully improves a deficient category
- the replacement protects fragile points in ERA, WHIP, or OBP
- the current active player has materially worse playing time or role outlook for the week
- the replacement has a superior weekly schedule or clearer path to stats

## 8. Final Lock Check

Before Sunday at 12:00 AM, confirm:

- all required active positions are filled
- no inactive or injured player remains in an active slot if a better legal option exists
- IR placements are complete
- minors are compliant
- bench and active units reflect the week's category priorities
- any free agent moves have been processed

Once complete, the roster is ready for weekly lock.

## Team-by-Team Sunday Checklist

Use this checklist for each of the 10 teams:

1. Review current standings by category
2. Mark categories as strong, competitive, or deficient
3. Review injuries and move eligible players to IR
4. Remove unavailable players from active lineup consideration
5. Check minors eligibility for every farm player
6. Review recent performance and role changes for active and bench players
7. Compare weak roster spots against available free agents
8. Add or replace players only where projected weekly value improves the team
9. Set active hitters based on games, role, OBP, speed, and power needs
10. Set active pitchers based on innings strategy, ratio protection, saves, and strikeout or win opportunity
11. Recheck roster legality
12. Save final lineup before the Sunday lock deadline

## Monday Morning Results Checkpoint

After the scoring week closes and before making new strategic judgments from the table, update the completed week's results.

### Monday Objective

Convert the prior scoring week's active-lineup output into refreshed season totals and updated standings for all 10 teams.

### Monday Order of Operations

1. Identify the active lineup that was locked for the completed scoring week
2. Pull the prior week's player stats for all active hitters and pitchers
3. Add those stats to each team's cumulative season totals
4. Recalculate league totals for all 10 roto categories
5. Re-rank teams within each category
6. Recompute total hitting points, pitching points, and overall roto points
7. Update the published standings view and any downstream summary files
8. Run the minor-league eligibility audit and flag any minors who must be promoted or cut before the next lock
9. Verify the refreshed standings and compliance report before using them for the next Sunday decision cycle

### Monday Validation Checklist

For each weekly standings refresh, confirm:

- only players who were active during the completed scoring week received stats
- bench and minors players were not credited unless they were activated before lock
- hitter totals and pitcher totals both roll forward correctly
- category leaders and trailing teams changed only where the new weekly stats justify it
- team point totals equal the sum of category points
- published standings and team summaries use the same updated totals
- any minor leaguer who crossed 130 MLB at-bats or 50 MLB innings is surfaced for a required roster move before the next lock
- each forced roster move includes a recommended promote-vs-drop decision and a projected starter-or-bench label if promoted

## Decision Standard

All Sunday roster decisions should be made independently for each team using the same process and the same evidence standard.

The objective is not to force identical strategy across teams. The objective is to maximize that specific team's expected scoring output for the coming week while preserving long-term dynasty value where reasonable.
