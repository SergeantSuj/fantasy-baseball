# Manager Profiles

This document is the index for the distinct operating profiles of each of the 10 teams in the league. These profiles are intended to guide Sunday roster decisions and long-term roster behavior while keeping every team competitive and internally consistent.

Use these profiles alongside [sunday-workflow.md](sunday-workflow.md) and [dynasty-league-rules.md](dynasty-league-rules.md).

Each profile influences:

- overall roster-construction behavior
- source trust hierarchy during close calls
- patience with slumping players
- willingness to hold injured players
- veteran versus prospect preference
- appetite for carrying minor-league stashes
- preferred prospect depth from upper minors versus deep developmental levels
- aggressiveness in free-agent replacement
- lineup flexibility for weekly category targeting
- tolerance for short-term risk versus long-term dynasty value

The goal is not to make any team irrational. The goal is to make each team behave differently while still trying to maximize its own results.

## Source Weighting Rule

All managers use the same core board, but they should not trust every input equally when the decision is close.

The primary source inputs available to shape manager behavior are:

- dynasty rankings
- market ADP
- projection data
- player context such as role, injury, and playing time
- skill indicators
- prospect-specific information when available

Manager personality should show up mostly in how these signals are prioritized inside a tier.

## Cross-Team Usage Rules

When using these profiles during Sunday roster management or long-term roster planning:

1. Always keep each team legal under the league rules.
2. Always try to improve each team's expected scoring outlook.
3. Apply the same information standard to every team.
4. Let the manager profile affect close decisions, not obvious ones.
5. Do not force bad moves just to create personality differences.
6. Let each manager's profile influence roster construction, risk tolerance, and prospect timing when choices are otherwise close.

## Close-Decision Guidance

Use manager profiles primarily when the decision is close, such as:

- whether to keep starting a slumping veteran
- whether to cut a low-performing bench player for a short-term free agent
- whether to activate a prospect now or wait another week
- whether to chase steals or saves with a specialist
- whether to protect OBP, ERA, and WHIP versus chasing volume
- whether to prefer a stable veteran versus a volatile upside play
- whether to prioritize prospects versus immediate MLB production

If one option is clearly superior for a team's weekly or long-term outcome, choose the superior option regardless of profile.

Greg is the veteran-leaning optimizer. He trusts established roles, values predictable production, and does not like carrying too many volatile prospects on the active roster.

### Tendencies

- Patience with poor performance: High for proven veterans, low for fringe players
- Veteran versus youth balance: Veteran leaning
- Category adjustment aggression: Moderate
- Free-agent activity: Opportunistic, mostly for reliable contributors
- Injury handling: Quick to replace short-term missing production
- Dynasty horizon: Win-now leaning

### Sunday Decision Style

- defaults to established MLB regulars over unproven upside plays
- promotes prospects only when playing time and role appear real
- uses specialists when needed, but prefers dependable weekly volume
- is more likely to cut a weak bench bat than a proven veteran in a slump

## Michael

### Profile Summary

Michael is an aggressive category chaser. He closely watches standings movement and is willing to reshape the active lineup to squeeze points from specific weak categories.

### Tendencies

- Patience with poor performance: Low to moderate
- Veteran versus youth balance: Flexible, performance-driven
- Category adjustment aggression: High
- Free-agent activity: High
- Injury handling: Very active
- Dynasty horizon: Short to medium term

### Sunday Decision Style

- quickly swaps lineup pieces when stolen bases, saves, or OBP can be improved
- will stream or rotate players more aggressively than most managers
- tolerates short-term churn if it creates a better weekly category outlook
- is comfortable benching a bigger name if the schedule or role is poor for that week

## Paul

### Profile Summary

Paul is the patient dynasty developer. He values long-term asset growth, believes in skill indicators beneath surface results, and is slower to abandon young talent.

### Tendencies

- Patience with poor performance: High, especially for young players
- Veteran versus youth balance: Youth leaning
- Category adjustment aggression: Low to moderate
- Free-agent activity: Measured
- Injury handling: Willing to stash meaningful long-term assets
- Dynasty horizon: Long term

### Sunday Decision Style

- gives prospects and early-career hitters extra time before demotion or replacement
- avoids sacrificing long-term upside for marginal one-week gains
- will still optimize the active lineup, but protects core young assets
- uses veterans mainly when they provide clear category stability or ratio protection

## Shane

### Profile Summary

Shane is the pragmatic grinder. He is detail-oriented, values weekly opportunity, and reacts quickly to role changes without becoming reckless.

### Tendencies

- Patience with poor performance: Moderate to low
- Veteran versus youth balance: Slight veteran lean for active lineup, youth lean on bench and minors
- Category adjustment aggression: High
- Free-agent activity: Active
- Injury handling: Efficient and unsentimental
- Dynasty horizon: Medium term

### Sunday Decision Style

- actively exploits two-start pitchers, temporary closers, and hitters moving up in batting order
- treats the bench as a working optimization tool rather than a stash area
- makes category-driven switches often, especially in saves, strikeouts, and OBP
- cuts low-utility bench pieces faster than the more patient teams

## Josh V

### Profile Summary

Josh V is the upside investor. He likes ceiling, embraces volatility, and is more willing than most to carry emerging players who could become long-term impact assets.

### Tendencies

- Patience with poor performance: Moderate for upside players, low for replaceable veterans
- Veteran versus youth balance: Youth and upside leaning
- Category adjustment aggression: Moderate
- Free-agent activity: Active when upside exists
- Injury handling: Will stash high-upside players longer than average
- Dynasty horizon: Long term with opportunistic pushes

### Sunday Decision Style

- gives breakout candidates more chances than steady low-ceiling veterans
- is willing to activate an emerging player earlier than a conservative manager would
- prioritizes long-term gain when lineup choices are close
- still makes weekly moves, but prefers upside-enhancing moves over low-impact churn

## Rob

### Profile Summary

Rob is the ratio-conscious risk manager. He is especially careful with OBP, ERA, and WHIP, and he avoids unnecessary weekly volatility when category damage could outweigh gains.

### Tendencies

- Patience with poor performance: Moderate
- Veteran versus youth balance: Balanced, with preference for skill stability
- Category adjustment aggression: Selective
- Free-agent activity: Moderate
- Injury handling: Conservative when protecting ratio categories
- Dynasty horizon: Medium term

### Sunday Decision Style

- benches risky low-OBP bats more readily than other managers
- is careful with fringe starters who could hurt ERA and WHIP
- prefers controlled gains over aggressive category gambles
- adds relievers or safer bats when protecting standings matters more than raw volume

## Wendell

### Profile Summary

Wendell is the traditionalist. He likes known quantities, leans on track record, and is slower to make sweeping changes unless the standings clearly demand it.

### Tendencies

- Patience with poor performance: High
- Veteran versus youth balance: Veteran leaning
- Category adjustment aggression: Low
- Free-agent activity: Low to moderate
- Injury handling: Patient with core players, slower on marginal replacements
- Dynasty horizon: Medium term with stability bias

### Sunday Decision Style

- keeps trusted veterans active through moderate slumps
- makes fewer week-to-week lineup pivots than aggressive managers
- only pursues specialists when a category deficit becomes obvious
- is reluctant to churn the bottom of the roster without a clear upgrade

## Matt

### Profile Summary

Matt is the matchup exploiter. He is highly engaged in weekly context and willing to alter the active lineup aggressively based on expected games, role shifts, and category opportunities.

### Tendencies

- Patience with poor performance: Low
- Veteran versus youth balance: Neutral, heavily matchup-driven
- Category adjustment aggression: Very high
- Free-agent activity: Very active
- Injury handling: Immediate action
- Dynasty horizon: Medium term with strong weekly focus

### Sunday Decision Style

- aggressively swaps active players to chase marginal weekly edges
- values scheduled volume and role clarity over name value
- will rotate pitchers and hitters quickly when categories are tight
- is most likely to make uncomfortable but rational benching decisions

## Josh M

### Profile Summary

Josh M is the portfolio manager. He balances floor and ceiling carefully, values roster construction, and tends to make measured decisions that preserve both weekly competitiveness and long-term flexibility.

### Tendencies

- Patience with poor performance: Moderate
- Veteran versus youth balance: Balanced with slight upside lean
- Category adjustment aggression: Moderate to high
- Free-agent activity: Moderate to active
- Injury handling: Practical and roster-aware
- Dynasty horizon: Medium to long term

### Sunday Decision Style

- prefers not to overreact to one bad week, but will respond when trends become actionable
- makes category-based switches when there is a realistic path to roto-point gain
- is willing to hold a young asset if the long-term value clearly exceeds the short-term free-agent replacement
- treats bench spots as both protection and optionality, not just raw production slots
