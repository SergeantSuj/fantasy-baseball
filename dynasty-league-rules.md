# 10-Team Dynasty Rotisserie League Rules

## League Overview

- League type: Dynasty
- Teams: 10
- Format: Season-long rotisserie
- Player pool: Mixed MLB
- Platform style: Fully automated stat accumulation and standings
- Champion: Team with the most total roto points at the end of the season

## Scoring Format

This league uses a 5x5 rotisserie format. In each category, teams are ranked from first through tenth.

- 1st place in a category earns 10 points
- 2nd place earns 9 points
- 3rd place earns 8 points
- 4th place earns 7 points
- 5th place earns 6 points
- 6th place earns 5 points
- 7th place earns 4 points
- 8th place earns 3 points
- 9th place earns 2 points
- 10th place earns 1 point

Final standings are determined by total roto points across all 10 categories.

## Minor League Eligibility

A player is minor league eligible only after he has been drafted or signed by an MLB organization and has not exceeded either of these MLB thresholds:

- 130 at-bats
- 50 innings pitched

College players, high school players, and other amateurs who have not yet been acquired by an MLB organization are not eligible for a minor league roster slot.

Once a player exceeds the eligibility threshold, he must be promoted to an active or bench spot by the next scoring period or within 7 days, whichever is earlier.

## Lineup Management

- Lineups lock weekly
- Active rosters lock at the beginning of each scoring week on Sunday at 12:00 AM
- Once the weekly lock occurs, active lineup slots cannot be changed until the next scoring week begins
- Only active players accumulate stats
- Bench and minor league players do not earn stats unless promoted into active lineup slots
- Stats from the completed scoring week are posted to season totals and standings during the Monday morning update cycle

This league uses a two-checkpoint weekly cadence. Managers set active lineups on Sunday evening before the weekly lock deadline, and the prior week's active-player stats are added to the standings during the Monday morning update.

## Pitching Limits

To preserve fair roto balance and prevent exploitative roster builds, the league uses both a minimum and maximum innings requirement.

- Minimum innings pitched: 950
- Maximum innings pitched: 1400

If a team fails to reach the minimum innings threshold, commissioner review may apply to pitching-point eligibility. Once a team reaches the maximum threshold, additional pitching stats do not count.

## Waivers and Free Agency

- Waiver system: FAAB
- Seasonal FAAB budget: 1000
- Minimum bid: 0
- Recommended processing cadence: Once per week, aligned with the weekly roster-lock cycle
- Optional post-waiver free agency: commissioner setting

FAAB is the default acquisition system for free agents during the season.

## Trades

- Trades are allowed during the season and offseason
- Trade deadline: August 15
- Minor leaguers may be traded
- Future draft picks may be traded
- Trade review standard: commissioner review for collusion only

League veto voting is not used.

## Dynasty Retention

- Teams may keep 25 MLB players from season to season
- Teams may keep all 15 of their minor league players from season to season
- There is no annual MLB redraft
- Teams must be roster compliant by all preseason deadlines

### Offseason Keeper Structure

- MLB keepers are defined as players retained in active, bench, IL, or other MLB-designated roster status
- Before the annual first-year player draft, each team must cut down to 25 MLB keepers
- Teams may retain anywhere from 0 to 15 minor leaguers, up to the full minor league roster limit of 15
- Teams that retain fewer than 15 minor leaguers open minor league draft slots for replacement selections

## Startup Draft

- Draft type: Snake draft
- Draft pool: MLB and eligible minor league players together
- Draft order: Randomized before Round 1
- Third-round reversal: Optional commissioner setting

## Annual First-Year Player Draft

- Draft length: Up to 15 rounds
- Timing: Offseason
- Eligible pool: Newly drafted or signed players who are now under contract with an MLB organization, plus other players who newly qualify for minor league roster status under league rules
- Draft order: Reverse order of the prior season standings

### First-Year Player Draft Mechanics

- The draft is used only to fill open minor league slots
- A team that keeps all 15 of its existing minor leaguers does not make any selections
- A team that keeps fewer than 15 minor leaguers may draft only enough players to refill its minors up to 15 total
- The draft may run a maximum of 15 rounds, but many teams may stop selecting before that point once their minor league roster is full
- Teams are not required to make every available selection

## Offseason Roster Cycle

The annual offseason process works as follows:

1. Finalize league standings and rookie-draft order
2. Allow offseason trades of MLB players, minor leaguers, and draft picks
3. Require each team to declare its 25 MLB keepers before the first-year player draft
4. Allow each team to keep up to all 15 existing minor leaguers
5. Run the first-year player draft in reverse standings order for teams with open minor league slots
6. Open post-draft waivers and FAAB for unrostered eligible players
7. Require all teams to be fully roster-compliant before Opening Day

## Anti-Tanking and Competitive Integrity

- Teams must maintain legal rosters and competitive lineups throughout the season
- Intentional non-competition is not allowed
- Commissioner may intervene if a team repeatedly leaves invalid lineups or fails to field a legal roster
- A weighted draft lottery for non-contending teams is recommended if tanking becomes a concern

## Recommended Tiebreakers

If teams finish tied in total roto points, use the following tiebreakers in order:

1. Most category wins
2. Most total hitting roto points
3. Most total pitching roto points
4. Commissioner-declared random tiebreak method if still tied

## Operating Summary

This league is a 10-team mixed MLB dynasty roto league using 5x5 scoring with OBP instead of batting average. Teams compete across 10 season-long categories, set lineups on a weekly lock cycle beginning Sunday at midnight, post the completed week's active-player stats into season totals and updated standings on Monday morning, keep 25 MLB players and up to 15 minor leaguers each offseason, do not hold an annual MLB redraft, use FAAB for in-season pickups, and replenish open minor league spots through an annual first-year player draft in reverse-standings order.
